Steering Control and Head Unit Repair – Melbourne Case Study

A customer contacted MP Auto Mechanics after experiencing multiple infotainment system issues. The steering wheel audio controls were no longer functioning correctly and the head unit was behaving intermittently.


Modern vehicles rely heavily on communication between the steering wheel controls, multimedia system and vehicle electronics. Even a minor fault can affect multiple vehicle functions and significantly reduce driver convenience.

Customer Complaint

The customer reported several concerns:

  • Steering wheel audio buttons not responding.
  • Volume controls operating intermittently.
  • Head unit occasionally freezing.
  • Loss of multimedia functionality.
  • Poor user experience while driving.

Because modern infotainment systems communicate with multiple vehicle modules, a detailed inspection was required.

Initial Inspection

The vehicle underwent a complete inspection of the multimedia and steering wheel control systems.

The steering wheel controls were tested individually to verify operation while the head unit functionality was monitored.

Electrical connections, communication wiring and multimedia settings were checked before proceeding with deeper diagnostics.

Diagnostic Process

A systematic diagnostic procedure was carried out to identify the source of the fault.

  • Steering wheel controls tested.
  • Head unit functionality verified.
  • Electrical wiring inspected.
  • Connector condition checked.
  • Power supply circuits tested.
  • Ground connections inspected.
  • Vehicle communication systems verified.

Testing confirmed that the issue was affecting the communication between the steering wheel controls and the multimedia system.

Repair Procedure

The affected components were inspected in detail and the necessary repairs were completed.

Electrical connections were cleaned and tested, communication pathways verified and the multimedia system checked for proper operation.

After completing repairs, all steering wheel functions were tested to ensure correct operation with the head unit.

Results After Repair

Following completion of the repair, all functions operated correctly.

  • Steering wheel controls restored.
  • Volume controls functioning normally.
  • Stable head unit performance.
  • Improved multimedia operation.
  • Enhanced driving convenience.

The vehicle was road tested and all systems were verified before returning the vehicle to the customer.

Common Signs of Steering Control or Head Unit Problems

Many drivers experience infotainment issues without realising an underlying electrical fault may be present.

  • Steering wheel buttons not working.
  • Head unit freezing or restarting.
  • Volume controls malfunctioning.
  • Bluetooth connectivity issues.
  • Display screen faults.
  • Intermittent multimedia operation.

Prompt diagnosis can prevent further electrical issues and improve overall vehicle usability.

Importance of Professional Diagnostics

Modern infotainment systems are integrated with vehicle electronics and communication networks.

Professional diagnostic equipment allows faults to be identified accurately without unnecessary parts replacement, helping achieve a reliable repair.
