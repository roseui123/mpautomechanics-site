Audi Brake Repair – Melbourne Case Study

An Audi owner contacted MP Auto Mechanics after noticing vibration during braking and a reduction in overall braking comfort. The customer reported that the steering wheel would shake slightly when slowing from higher speeds and that the vehicle no longer felt as smooth under braking as it had previously.


Because braking performance is a critical safety system, a complete inspection was performed to identify the cause of the problem and determine the condition of the braking components.

Customer Complaint

The customer reported several concerns:

  • Steering wheel vibration during braking.
  • Brake pedal pulsation.
  • Reduced braking smoothness.
  • Occasional brake noise.
  • Concern about brake safety.

These symptoms commonly indicate excessive brake wear or rotor surface irregularities, requiring a detailed inspection.

Vehicle Inspection

The Audi was inspected to evaluate the condition of the brake system. Wheel assemblies were removed and the brake components examined for wear, heat damage and uneven contact surfaces.

Brake pad thickness was measured and the brake rotors were checked for signs of scoring, heat spots and uneven wear.

Additional inspection of suspension and steering components was also completed to ensure no other faults were contributing to the vibration concern.

Diagnostic Findings

Inspection identified excessive wear on braking components that was contributing to the vibration experienced during braking.

  • Brake rotor wear identified.
  • Uneven rotor surface condition.
  • Brake pad wear observed.
  • Heat-related rotor markings present.
  • No steering or suspension faults found.

The findings confirmed the issue originated within the braking system rather than the steering or suspension.

Repair Procedure

The brake system was serviced following professional repair procedures.

The affected brake components were removed and inspected. Mating surfaces were cleaned and prepared before reassembly.

Brake hardware, mounting points and surrounding components were inspected to ensure reliable operation after completion of repairs.

Once repairs were completed, the braking system was checked and road tested.

Results After Repair

Following repair, the Audi demonstrated significantly improved braking performance.

  • Brake vibration eliminated.
  • Smoother braking feel restored.
  • Improved driver confidence.
  • Reduced brake noise.
  • Consistent braking performance.

The customer reported a noticeable improvement in vehicle comfort and braking response.

Common Audi Brake Problems

Audi vehicles deliver excellent braking performance but, like all vehicles, braking components wear over time.

  • Brake rotor wear.
  • Brake pad wear.
  • Brake vibration.
  • Brake squeal.
  • Heat-related rotor damage.
  • Brake pedal pulsation.

Regular inspections help identify these concerns before they affect safety or create larger repair costs.

Why Brake Inspections Matter

Brake inspections allow worn components to be detected before they become major safety concerns.

Routine servicing helps maintain reliable braking performance, reduce repair costs and improve overall vehicle safety.

At MP Auto Mechanics, brake inspections include checking rotor condition, pad wear, brake hardware and overall system operation.
